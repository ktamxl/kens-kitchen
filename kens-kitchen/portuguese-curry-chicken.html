Portuguese Curry Chicken

葡國雞
Serves4
MarinateOvernight
Cook Time~45 min + 20 min oven
DifficultyIntermediate

This is the dish that Macau gave to the world — and the world never forgot it. Tender chicken in a golden, roux-thickened coconut curry gravy, finished in the oven with toasted shaved coconut. It tastes like the story of a city: Portuguese spice traders meeting Chinese ingredients across 500 years of shared history. Silky, fragrant, and deeply satisfying.

The Story Behind the Dish

How East met West in a curry pot

In 1557, Portuguese traders established a settlement in Macau — the first permanent European trading post in Asia. For more than 400 years, Portuguese sailors, merchants, and administrators made this tiny peninsula their home, bringing with them spices from India, techniques from the Mediterranean, and a taste for slow-cooked, aromatic stews.

What emerged from this centuries-long collision was Macanese cuisine — a unique hybrid found nowhere else on earth. 葡國雞 (Portuguese Chicken) is its crown jewel: a dish that could not have come from Portugal alone, nor from China alone. The turmeric and coconut cream are Southeast Asian, the technique of making a flour-butter roux is French via Portugal, and the preference for braised chicken with potatoes is pure home cooking, adapted to the local pantry.

By the 20th century, as Macanese families emigrated to Hong Kong, the dish traveled with them. It found its spiritual home in the 茶餐廳 (cha chaan teng) — Hong Kong's beloved working-class cafés that fused East and West on every table. 葡國雞 over rice became a lunch counter staple, its creamy golden sauce soaked up with crusty bread or steamed white rice.

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

Chicken, cut into pieces450g
Salt1 tsp
Sugar1 tbsp
Onion powder2 tsp
Garlic powder1 tsp
Curry powder½ tsp
Don't skip overnight: The sugar helps caramelise; the spices penetrate deeply. This is where the flavour foundation is built.

Vegetables

Onion200g (½ medium)
Potato500g (4 medium, chunked)
Carrot2 sticks, sliced
Bell pepper½, any colour
Shallot1 small, sliced
Shaved coconut2 tbsp (for topping)

Roux

All-purpose flour2 tbsp (50g)
Unsalted butter2 tbsp (½ stick)

Gravy

Turmeric powder1 tbsp
Curry powder½ tsp
Chicken stock1 can
Coconut cream½ can
Water½ can (use stock can to measure)
02

Instructions

  1. 1
    Marinate the Chicken
    Combine all marinade ingredients with the chicken pieces. Mix thoroughly so every surface is coated. Cover and refrigerate overnight. This step is the backbone of the dish's flavour.
  2. 2
    Prep the Vegetables
    Blanch carrots in boiling water until just tender — they should hold their shape. Pan-fry potato chunks in a little oil until golden on the outside (they don't need to cook through yet). Set both aside.
  3. 3
    Make the Roux
    Melt butter in a small pan over low heat. Whisk in flour and cook, stirring constantly, for 1–2 minutes until the mixture turns pale golden and smells slightly nutty. Remove from heat. This is the key to the silky, restaurant-quality finish.
  4. 4
    Sear the Chicken
    Heat a pan with a little oil and lightly sear the marinated chicken pieces until browned on the outside. Set aside — it does not need to be fully cooked at this stage. The oven will finish the job.
  5. 5
    Build the Gravy Base
    In the same pan, add a little oil and stir-fry the shallots, onion, and bell pepper until soft and fragrant. Pour in the gravy mixture (turmeric, curry powder, chicken stock, coconut cream, and water). Stir and bring to a simmer.
  6. 6
    Thicken with Roux
    Add the roux to the simmering gravy. Lower heat and stir until the gravy becomes smooth, creamy, and coats the back of a spoon. Don't rush — this is what separates a great 葡國雞 from an ordinary curry.
  7. 7
    Combine Everything
    Add the seared chicken, blanched carrots, and pan-fried potatoes to the gravy. Stir gently to coat. Allow everything to return to a gentle simmer.
  8. 8
    Oven Finish
    Transfer everything to an oven-safe baking dish. Sprinkle shaved coconut evenly across the top. Bake at 400°F / 200°C for 20 minutes until the coconut is toasted golden and the chicken is fully cooked through. Serve immediately over steamed rice or with crusty bread.

Chef's Notes

🧈

The Roux Is Non-Negotiable

This is what makes the gravy silky and restaurant-quality. Don't skip it or substitute with cornstarch — the flavour is different.

🥥

Coconut Cream vs Milk

Coconut cream gives a richer, more luxurious result. Substitute coconut milk for a lighter gravy — both work, different personalities.

🌡️

Oven Time Doesn't Scale

When scaling for a bigger crowd, only increase ingredient quantities. Oven temp and bake time stay the same regardless of batch size.

🍚

Serving

Best over plain jasmine rice — the contrast of white rice and golden curry is the point. Crusty bread also works beautifully for sauce-soaking.

Scaling This Recipe

Scale all ingredient quantities proportionally. Oven temp (400°F/200°C) and baking time (20 min) do not change.
